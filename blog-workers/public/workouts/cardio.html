Cardio Training

Cardio (aerobic exercise) strengthens your heart and lungs, boosts endurance, and supports fat loss when combined with strength training and good nutrition.

What is cardio?

Steady-state cardio keeps your heart rate elevated for 20–60 minutes: running, cycling, swimming, rowing, brisk walking, or dancing. You should be able to speak in short sentences — the "talk test."

Key benefits

  • Stronger cardiovascular system
  • Better stamina for sports and daily life
  • Improved mood and stress relief
  • Supports healthy weight management

Sample beginner week

  • Mon: 25 min brisk walk
  • Wed: 20 min easy cycling
  • Fri: 30 min walk with hills
  • Weekend: Active rest — hike or light swim

How to start safely

  1. Start with 15–20 minutes if you are new
  2. Increase duration by 5–10% per week
  3. Wear supportive shoes for running
  4. Stay hydrated before, during, and after
  5. Pair cardio with 2–3 strength sessions weekly
Heart rate guide: Moderate cardio = roughly 50–70% of max heart rate. A simple estimate: 220 minus your age.

How often?

Aim for 150 minutes of moderate cardio per week (WHO guideline) — about 30 minutes, 5 days a week. More is fine if you recover well.
