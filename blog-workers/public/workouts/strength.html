Strength Training

Build muscle, bone density, and functional power with resistance training. Strength work is the foundation of almost every fitness goal — from looking toned to moving better in daily life.

What is strength training?

Strength training uses resistance — barbells, dumbbells, machines, bands, or bodyweight — to challenge your muscles. Typical sets use 3–6 sets of 5–12 reps with controlled form.

Key benefits

  • Increased muscle mass and metabolism
  • Stronger bones and joints
  • Better posture and injury resilience
  • Improved performance in sports and daily tasks

Sample beginner workout (45 min)

  1. Warm-up: 5–8 min walk + arm circles + bodyweight squats
  2. Goblet squat — 3 × 10
  3. Push-ups — 3 × 8–12
  4. Dumbbell row — 3 × 10 each arm
  5. Glute bridge — 3 × 12
  6. Plank — 3 × 30 sec
  7. Cool-down stretch — 5 min

Form tips

  • Brace your core before each rep
  • Control the lowering phase (2–3 seconds)
  • Keep spine neutral — no rounding on deadlifts or rows
  • Rest 60–90 seconds between sets (longer for heavy lifts)
Pro tip: Add a small amount of weight or 1–2 reps each week. Progressive overload is how muscles grow.

How often?

Train each muscle group 2× per week with at least 48 hours rest between sessions. A 3-day full-body split works great for beginners.
