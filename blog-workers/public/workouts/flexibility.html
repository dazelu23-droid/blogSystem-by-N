Flexibility & Mobility

Flexibility training improves range of motion, reduces stiffness, and helps prevent injury. Mobility work keeps joints healthy so you can squat, reach, and move without pain.

What is flexibility training?

Static stretching holds a muscle lengthened for 20–60 seconds. Dynamic mobility uses controlled movement through full range: leg swings, cat-cow, world's greatest stretch. Yoga combines both with strength and balance.

Key benefits

  • Less muscle tightness and better posture
  • Improved performance in strength and cardio
  • Faster recovery between hard workouts
  • Lower injury risk when paired with strength training

10-minute daily mobility routine

  1. Cat-cow — 10 reps
  2. World's greatest stretch — 5 each side
  3. Leg swings — 10 front/back, 10 side each leg
  4. Deep squat hold — 30 sec
  5. Thread the needle — 30 sec each side
  6. Hamstring stretch — 30 sec each leg

When to stretch

  • Dynamic mobility — before workouts (warm-up)
  • Static stretching — after workouts or before bed
  • Yoga sessions — 1–3× per week on rest days
Don't bounce: Hold static stretches steady. Bouncing can trigger the stretch reflex and increase injury risk.

How often?

5–10 minutes daily mobility plus 1–2 longer yoga or stretch sessions per week. Consistency beats occasional long sessions.
