HIIT — High-Intensity Interval Training

HIIT alternates short bursts of maximum effort with rest periods. It is time-efficient, burns calories quickly, and improves both aerobic and anaerobic fitness.

What is HIIT?

You work at 80–95% effort for 20–60 seconds, then rest or move lightly for 1–2 minutes. Repeat 6–12 rounds. Total session: 15–30 minutes including warm-up.

Key benefits

  • Maximum calorie burn in minimum time
  • Boosts metabolism for hours after training (EPOC)
  • Improves speed and power
  • No equipment required for many routines

Beginner bodyweight circuit (20 min)

  1. Warm-up: 5 min jumping jacks + dynamic stretches
  2. 40 sec work / 20 sec rest each: squats, push-ups, mountain climbers, lunges
  3. Repeat circuit 3 times
  4. Cool-down: 5 min walk + stretch

Safety rules

  • Build a cardio base before going all-out
  • Land softly on jumps — protect knees
  • Stop if you feel dizzy or sharp pain
  • Limit HIIT to 2–3 sessions per week
  • Always include a proper warm-up
Effort check: During work intervals you should not be able to hold a conversation. If you can, push harder (if safe) or shorten rest.

How often?

2–3 HIIT sessions per week with at least one rest day between. Combine with strength training and easy cardio on other days.
